Before You Request Access

The more context you share, the more useful the first review can be.

This short registration helps us understand whether you are researching a future exchange, working inside the 45-day identification window, or evaluating passive real-estate income outside an exchange. You may be asked about investment amount, debt replacement, property types, geography, timing, and goals so that the information we show is relevant to the conversation.

Registration does not create an account approval, guarantee access to an offering, or make an investment recommendation. Current offerings are private placements for accredited investors and are made solely through the sponsor's Private Placement Memorandum. The PPM and its supplements control the terms, fees, projected income, risk factors, and suitability process.

Keep your qualified intermediary, CPA, attorney, and financial adviser involved. Baker 1031 can help organize questions and compare published materials, but it does not provide tax or legal advice and cannot determine whether a transaction is right for your circumstances.

After submission, the desk may follow up to confirm contact information, accreditation and suitability details, exchange timing, and the kinds of materials you want to review. We may recommend starting with a guide or a document review rather than a particular offering. Access to information is not a commitment to invest, and completing the form does not extend the 45-day or 180-day exchange deadlines.

Only share information needed for the conversation. Do not send tax returns, account passwords, Social Security numbers, wire instructions, or other sensitive documents through this form. If a document is needed later, the desk will explain the appropriate secure process.
