Hospitality

Hotels — higher potential yield with daily-repricing volatility.

Hospitality covers hotels and resorts, where “leases” reset every night. Income can be higher than other property types, but so is volatility.

Risk note. Hospitality is among the most volatile property types. Income depends on nightly demand and can decline rapidly in a downturn; reserves and operator quality are critical.

Why Hospitality in a DST

Daily room repricing means hotels can capture inflation and demand spikes quickly, and strong brands and locations can drive outsized performance in good markets.

Key risks

  • RevPAR volatility across the economic cycle — income can swing sharply.
  • Brand/flag and franchise-agreement obligations.
  • Recurring capital and property-improvement-plan (PIP) costs.
  • Management dependency and operating leverage.

Diligence focus

  • Brand/flag, franchise terms, and PIP obligations.
  • RevPAR versus competitive set and market trends.
  • Management company track record.
  • Capital reserves and renovation timing.

Frequently asked questions

Why are hotels riskier than other DSTs?
Hotel “leases” reset nightly, so revenue reacts immediately to economic shocks, travel disruption, and seasonality — creating higher potential return and higher risk of loss.
Can hotels qualify for a 1031 exchange through a DST?
Yes — hotel real estate held in a DST is like-kind property under Revenue Ruling 2004-86, though the operating business is typically held through a master-lease structure.
What should I focus on when evaluating a hotel DST?
Brand and flag, local demand drivers, the strength of the operator or master lessee, and how leverage is structured, since cash flow can swing sharply with occupancy.
