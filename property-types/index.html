Property Types

The real estate behind a DST.

A 1031 exchange is only as sound as the property underneath it. These are the commercial real estate types we cover — each with a different return, risk, and management profile. Understand the asset before the structure.

Multifamily
Apartment communities — the deepest, most institutionally covered slice of the DST market.
Illustrative cash-on-cash: ~4.5%–5.5%
Net-Lease Retail
Single-tenant, essential retail on long leases — the classic passive-income structure.
Illustrative cash-on-cash: ~6.0%–6.75%
Industrial & Logistics
Warehouse, distribution, and logistics space riding the e-commerce supply chain.
Illustrative cash-on-cash: ~5.0%–5.75%
Medical & Healthcare
Medical office and healthcare real estate anchored by demographic demand.
Illustrative cash-on-cash: ~5.5%–6.5%
Self-Storage
Granular, quick-to-reprice leases with low operating cost and resilience.
Illustrative cash-on-cash: ~5.0%–6.0%
Manufactured Housing
Affordable-housing communities with low turnover and durable demand.
Illustrative cash-on-cash: ~5.0%–6.0%
Senior Living
Demographically driven housing and care — higher operating dependency.
Illustrative cash-on-cash: ~6.0%–7.0%
Student Housing
Purpose-built housing serving enrollment-driven university demand.
Illustrative cash-on-cash: ~5.0%–6.0%
Hospitality
Hotels — higher potential yield with daily-repricing volatility.
Illustrative cash-on-cash: Higher / variable
Office
Income-producing office — attractive yields against real secular headwinds.
Illustrative cash-on-cash: Higher (risk-adj.)
Data Centers
Digital infrastructure powering cloud and AI demand.
Illustrative cash-on-cash: ~5.0%–6.0%
